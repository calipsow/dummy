# SoFi Technologies, Inc. Q1 2026 Financial Report Summary

_[Read Original Filing](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1818874/000181887426000037/sofi-20260331.htm)_

## I. Financial Highlights (For the quarter ended March 31, 2026)
Total net revenue reached $1.1 billion, representing a 43% increase compared to the first quarter of 2025.

Net income was $166.7 million, a 134% increase from $71.1 million in the same period of the prior year.

Diluted earnings per share (EPS) stood at $0.12, double the $0.06 reported in Q1 2025.

Adjusted EBITDA (a non-GAAP measure) was $339.9 million, an increase of 62% year-over-year.

Net interest income grew 39% year-over-year to $693.0 million, driven by growth in average loan balances and deposits.

## II. Key Business Metrics (As of March 31, 2026)
Total members grew to over 14.7 million, a 35% year-over-year increase.

Total products on the platform reached nearly 22.2 million, a 39% year-over-year increase.

Lending segment products increased by 33% to 2.8 million.

Financial Services segment products grew by 40% to 19.3 million.

Technology Platform total enabled client accounts were 133 million, a 16% decrease from the prior year, reflecting the exit of a large client.

## III. Segment Performance (Q1 2026 vs. Q1 2025)

Lending Segment: 
The segment's total net revenue increased by 55% to $642.4 million.

Contribution profit grew by 60% to $382.4 million.

Total origination volume was $12.2 billion, a 68% increase, fueled by strong demand across personal, student, and home loans.

Technology Platform Segment: 
Net revenue was $75.1 million, a 27% decrease from the prior year. This decline was primarily due to a large client fully transitioning off the platform in 2025.

Contribution profit was $12.0 million, down 61%.

Financial Services Segment: Net revenue grew by 41% to $428.5 million.

Contribution profit increased by 32% to $195.6 million.

Key drivers included $138.3 million in fees from the Loan Platform Business, a 54% increase in interchange revenue, and a 116% increase in brokerage revenue.

## IV. Financial Condition and Liquidity
Total assets as of March 31, 2026, were $53.7 billion, an increase of 6% from year-end 2025.

Total deposits grew to $40.2 billion, an increase of $2.7 billion during the quarter. Approximately 97% of total deposits were insured.

Total liabilities stood at $42.9 billion, and total equity increased to $10.8 billion.

The company and SoFi Bank remain well-capitalized, with total risk-based capital ratios of 21.3% and 15.4%, respectively, exceeding regulatory requirements.

## V. New Developments and Risk Factors
The company launched SoFi Crypto in late 2025 and its proprietary stablecoin, SoFiUSD, in the first quarter of 2026.

New risk factors were disclosed, including risks associated with the complex regulatory environment and operational demands of the SoFiUSD stablecoin, 
and potential liabilities from a new self-insurance model for employee medical benefits.
